Operation Sindoor :

ü™î What is Operation Sindoor?
Operation Sindoor is a high-precision, multi-phase Indian military operation launched on 7 May 2025 in retaliation for the Pahalgam terror attack that occurred on 22 April 2025.
It aimed to eliminate terrorist launchpads and leadership operating in Pakistan and Pakistan-occupied Kashmir (PoK).

üéØ Why Was It Launched?
On April 22, 2025, 17 civilians were killed and 43 injured in a brutal terror attack in Pahalgam, Jammu and Kashmir. The attack was attributed to Jaish-e-Mohammed (JeM) and Lashkar-e-Taiba (LeT).
India‚Äôs response was decisive: to neutralize the terror ecosystem fueling cross-border strikes.

‚öîÔ∏è How Was Operation Sindoor Executed?
Phase 1: RAW, NTRO, and Army Intelligence tracked terror hubs via drones, satellites, and field agents.
Phase 2: Airstrikes began at 03:20 AM IST using Rafale, Mirage 2000, and Tejas jets.
Phase 3: Loitering munitions and drones destroyed infrastructure in Kotli, Bahawalpur, Muridke.
Phase 4: Indian Navy sealed maritime movement; Army artillery supported from LoC.
Phase 5: All jets returned safe. Ceasefire requested by Pakistan on 10 May 2025.

üìç Where Did the Strikes Happen?
The strikes Time: Conducted between 1:05 AM and 1:30 AM.
In Pakistan: Bahawalpur, Muridke, Sialkot, Chak Amru
In PoK: Muzaffarabad, Bhimber, Kotli, Gulpur
These were known terror breeding zones and command centers of LeT and JeM.
