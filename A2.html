Why Operation Sindoor?

Operation Sindoor was conceived as a necessary and urgent military response to escalating cross-border threats. The enemy had been actively using terror camps and weapons depots in Pakistan-controlled areas to prepare attacks on Indian soil.

Credible intelligence confirmed the presence of high-value targets ‚Äî including foreign-trained militants, advanced weaponry, and active command centers. Delaying action would have risked national security and allowed future terror strikes.

Strategic Justification

The mission was essential to:

  • Eliminate imminent terror threats before they materialized.
  • Destroy enemy infrastructure that supported cross-border terrorism.
  • Disrupt terror financing and logistics chains.
  • Send a strong deterrent signal to hostile forces.

Moral & Legal Rationale

India acted within the framework of international law and self-defense. The operation followed months of provocations, infiltration attempts, and loss of innocent civilian lives to terror attacks.

Operation Sindoor was not an act of aggression ‚Äî it was a measured defensive strike designed to ensure peace and stability in the region.

Timing of the Operation

The strike was conducted at a moment when maximum intelligence clarity and tactical advantage could be achieved ‚Äî minimizing risk to civilians while ensuring target destruction.
